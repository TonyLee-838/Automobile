Fluid Mechanics

Viscosity (粘度)

  • measure of a fluid's resistance to flow, which determines the fluid strain rate that's generated by the given shear stress.
  • Viscosity Coefficient μ\muμ are given by
    τ=μdudy\tau = \mu \frac{du}{dy}τ=μdydu​
    which uuu is moving speed of upper surface.
    • The linear fluids that follow the equation above are called newtonian fluid.
    • The viscosity of newtonian fluid is a thermodynamic property and varies with temperature and pressure. But generally, viscosity grows weakly with pressure but strongly with temperature.

Kinematic Viscosity

  • Definition:
    ν=μρ\nu = \frac{\mu}{\rho}ν=ρμ​

Hydrostatic

Hydrostatic Condition:

  1. Pressure in a static fluid varies only with vertical distance, and it's independent of the shape of the container.
  2. The pressure is the same at all points on a given horizontal plane in the fluid.
  3. The pressure increase with DEPTH in the fluid.

Hydrostatic in Liquids:

  • Liquids are nearly incompressible. Their density merely increase as going depth. Thus, we assume there's constant density in liquid hydrostatic calculations
    p2−p1=ρg(z1−z2)p_2 - p_1 = \rho g (z_1 - z_2)p2​−p1​=ρg(z1​−z2​)
    Or
    z1−z2=p2ρg−p1ρgz_1 - z_2 = \frac{p_2}{\rho g} - \frac{p_1}{\rho g}z1​−z2​=ρgp2​​−ρgp1​​

    The quantity p/ρgp / \rho gp/ρg is a length called the pressure head of the fluid.
    And we give an allies γ\gammaγ with the value γ=ρg\gamma = \rho gγ=ρg to simplify the expression.

Pascal's Law

Any two points at the same elevation (height) in a continuous mass of the same static fluid will be at the same pressure.

Two ways to solve fluid flow problems --- eulerian frame and lagrangian frame

  • Total acceleration (aaa): a=∂u∂t+(u∂V∂x+v∂V∂y+w∂V∂z)a = \frac{∂u}{∂t} + (u\frac{∂V}{∂x} + v\frac{∂V}{∂y} + w\frac{∂V}{∂z})a=∂t∂u​+(u∂x∂V​+v∂y∂V​+w∂z∂V​)
    where ∂u∂t\frac{∂u}{∂t}∂t∂u​ is called local acceleration, which vanishes if the flow is steady (independent of time, 定常流動), and the others are called convective acceleration.
